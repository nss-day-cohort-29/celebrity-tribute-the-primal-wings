The Life of the Man in Black

Johnny Cash was born on February 26, 1932 in Kingsland, Arkansas (United States). He was one of 8 children, and his parents were Ray and Carrie Cash. He was raised on a farm in Dyess Colony, and worked the fields with his siblings through most of childhood. He graduated in 1950, and worked at an automotive plant in Pontiac, Michigan until he joined the Air Force. He was based in Landsburg, Germany, where he met and married his first wife, Vivian Liberto after his discharge in 1954.

He began his solo career and eventually met and married June Carter. June and Johnny have seven children.

Johnny was referred to as the man in black simply referred to his on-stage costume, which was an all black suit and his song "Man in Black." It stood for protesting the poor being treated wrongly by wealthy politicians, mass incarceration, and the war in Vietnam.
